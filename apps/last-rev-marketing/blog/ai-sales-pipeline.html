We were paying $150/month per seat for a CRM we barely used. Most of the features were irrelevant to a 15-person agency. What we actually needed was a clean pipeline view, deal tracking, and forecasting. So we canceled the subscription and asked AlphaClaw to build exactly that.

Why Off-the-Shelf Didn't Work

Enterprise CRMs are designed for enterprise sales teams. They assume you have SDRs, AEs, and sales managers with distinct workflows. Our sales process is simpler: a partner has a conversation, writes a proposal, negotiates, and closes. We didn't need 200 pipeline stages or automated cadence sequences.

What we needed was visibility into our pipeline value, weighted forecasting, and a way to track where each opportunity stood without opening five different tabs.

The Build

AlphaClaw created a focused pipeline management tool in about an hour. The interface centers on a horizontal pipeline view with four stages: Discovery, Proposal, Negotiation, and Closed. Deals are cards that show company name, estimated value, probability, and days in stage.

The AI added a forecasting panel that calculates weighted pipeline value — multiplying each deal's value by its probability — giving us a realistic revenue forecast instead of the inflated "total pipeline" number that CRMs love to show.

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop pipeline with automatic stage-change timestamps and history
  • Weighted forecasting — real-time revenue projections based on probability and deal value
  • Deal velocity tracking — average days per stage with bottleneck highlighting
  • Win/loss analysis — historical view of closed deals with reasons and patterns
  • Weekly digest — auto-generated summary of pipeline changes, new deals, and stalled opportunities

The Unexpected Benefit

Because the tool was so simple, people actually used it. Our previous CRM had terrible adoption because updating it felt like a chore. This tool has three fields per deal and takes 10 seconds to update. Usage went from sporadic to daily across the team.

AlphaClaw also built in a "stale deal" detector that flags any opportunity with no activity in 14 days. This simple feature surfaced three deals that had gone cold without anyone noticing — one of which we re-engaged and eventually closed for $85K.

Results

We saved $1,800/year in CRM costs and got a tool that better fits our workflow. Pipeline visibility went from a monthly spreadsheet review to real-time access. Forecast accuracy improved because people actually kept the data current.
